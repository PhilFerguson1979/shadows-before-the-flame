# Shared Tales: Legends of the Fellowship

These are the moments the party remembers when they raise a mug to Ispin's memory.

---

## The Beast of Lemon-Brook

**The Bond:** Trust and Mercy

> "Remember the bridge at Lemon-Brook? We were hired to 'slay the beast' that was terrorizing the local trade route. We went in with weapons drawn, expecting a monster out of a nightmare, only to find that massive Owlbear mother. She wasn't evil — she was in pain. While the rest of us held her down, straining against a thousand pounds of muscle and fur, Ispin didn't reach for his sword. He reached for his tongs. He pulled that black-iron thorn from her paw while we held our breath, praying our strength wouldn't fail. We learned that day that not every monster needs a blade; sometimes, the greatest strength is knowing when *not* to kill."

**Inside Joke:** To this day, the group still argues over who actually had the best grip on the bear's neck.

---

## The Festival of the Eye

**The Bond:** Mischief and Wit

> "The High Seekers always hated that Ispin brought 'troublemakers' to the Festival of the Eye, but someone had to stop that con artist from fleecing the locals. Remember the 'Great Dice Swap'? The Bard had the crowd in the palm of their hand, the Rogue moved like a shadow to replace the loaded bones, and the magic-users made sure the cheater's dignity — and his trousers — hit the dirt at the exact same time. We didn't just save the town's copper; we bonded over the secret joy of out-cheating a cheater."

**Inside Joke:** "Never trust a man with two left dice."

---

## The Storm on the Road to Gateway

**The Bond:** Shared Hardship

> "The job was supposed to be a simple caravan escort to Haven, but the sky had other plans. That storm on the road to Gateway was the worst Krynn had seen in a decade. When the wagon got mired axle-deep in that black mud, we didn't have heroes or commoners — just ten shivering souls in the pouring rain.
>
> Remember eventually we were surrounded by those starving goblins — not warriors, just desperate scavengers. Ispin stood in the middle of the road, shield held high, not striking a single blow. He just talked them down. We spent the night huddled under a single, leaking tarp, sharing cold rations and the warmth of each other's shoulders. That was the night Ispin told us that steel isn't always the answer, but having it ready makes the talking easier."

**The Bond:** You didn't just push a wagon that night; you became the wall that kept the world at bay.

---

## The Bitter Cold of the Peaks

**The Bond:** Becoming Family

> "Four winters ago, we were trapped by the early snows in the Kharolis foothills. We had one fire, three blankets, and enough dried meat for maybe two days. We took turns staying awake in that freezing dark, rubbing each other's hands to keep the frostbite away, making sure no one slipped into that final sleep. That was the night we stopped being 'hired swords' and became a family. We swore then that no matter where the winds took us, we'd meet back at the Inn of the Last Home. We survived because we refused to let the fire go out."

**The Vow:** "No matter the distance, the Inn is home."
